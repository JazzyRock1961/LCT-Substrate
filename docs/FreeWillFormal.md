Proof of Free Will: 

The Non-Locality Reductio  

1. Definition of Propositions  
S: The subvenient base substrate exists and operates strictly via local state transitions.  
P: The properties of these subvenient state transitions are stochastic (governed by a random probability distribution at the localized level).  
M: The supervenient mathematics of the mind determines its own macro-states.  
D: Subvenient determinism is globally true (the macro-state is entirely dictated by the subvenient evolution).  K: The system possesses non-local informational access (the ability for local operators to access or process non-local macro-context).  

2. Formal Premises  
Premise 1: S → ¬K
(The subvenient substrate is strictly local; a localized logic switch cannot process non-local information.)  
Premise 2: S
(The subvenient substrate exists and executes its local operations.)  
Premise 3: P ∧ M
(The subvenient layer exhibits stochastic properties, and a self-determining supervenient mind layer emerges over it.)  
Premise 4: (P ∧ M ∧ D) → K
(The Thesis: For determinism to successfully bridge the stochastic gap and account for supervenient mathematical choices, the subvenient layer would require non-local informational access.)  

3. Deductive Derivation (Reductio ad Absurdum)  ¬K (From Premise 1 and Premise 2 via Modus Ponens)  
Assume D (Temporary Assumption for Contradiction)  
P ∧ M ∧ D (From Premise 3 and line 2 via Conjunction introduced)  
K (From Premise 4 and line 3 via Modus Ponens)  
K ∧ ¬K (From line 4 and line 1 via Conjunction; Logical Contradiction)  ---∴ ¬D (By Reductio ad Absurdum; the assumption of determinism is rejected)  

4. Core Theorem  $$S \wedge (P \wedge M) \wedge ((P \wedge M \wedge D) \rightarrow K) \wedge (S \rightarrow \neg K) \vdash \neg D$$  

Conclusion: Because a discrete, local state-machine substrate cannot tolerate non-local informational access $(S \rightarrow \neg K)$, global determinism collapses under its own structural requirements $(\neg D)$. The supervenient mathematics of the mind is fundamentally self-determining within its internal resource limits and external relational boundaries.
